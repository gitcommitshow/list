What is the Data Privacy Framework?

The Data Privacy Framework (DPF) is a legal mechanism that allows US companies to transfer personal data from the EU, UK, and Switzerland to the United States in compliance with European data protection laws (GDPR).

Why it matters

Without DPF: Companies must negotiate complex Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for every EU partner, conduct Transfer Impact Assessments, and add 2-6 weeks to procurement cycles.

With DPF: Transfer data freely, streamline EU customer onboarding, and demonstrate verified compliance through public certification.

Real impact: When RudderStack achieved DPF certification (Nov 2025), they eliminated the need to negotiate separate data transfer agreements for each European customer—a process that previously added weeks to enterprise sales cycles.

Three Frameworks

  • EU-U.S. DPF: For data from EU/EEA countries
  • UK Extension: For data from United Kingdom
  • Swiss-U.S. DPF: For data from Switzerland

Companies can certify for one, two, or all three frameworks. 74% of certified companies choose comprehensive coverage across all three.

Key Requirements

Eligibility

  • US legal entity
  • Under FTC or DOT jurisdiction
  • NOT: Banks, insurance, telecoms

7 Core Principles

  • Notice & Transparency
  • Individual Choice
  • Accountability for Transfers
  • Data Security
  • Data Integrity
  • Access Rights
  • Recourse & Enforcement

Privacy Policy

  • DPF participation statement
  • Link to public certification list
  • Data types covered (HR/Non-HR)
  • Individual rights explanation
  • Complaint mechanisms

Independent Recourse

  • Designate dispute resolution provider
  • Free for complainants
  • BBB National Programs (common)
  • JAMS or other approved providers

Annual Requirements

  • Re-certify every year
  • Pay annual fee ($250-$4,500)
  • Update privacy policy
  • Maintain compliance

Verification

  • Self-assessment, OR
  • Outside compliance review
  • Document compliance annually
  • Optional: "DPF Verified" seal

Certification Checklist

Follow these steps to achieve DPF certification (typically 4-6 weeks)

1. Confirm Eligibility

Verify your company is subject to FTC or DOT jurisdiction. Check that you're not a bank, insurance company, or telecommunications provider.

2. Conduct Internal Assessment

Review your current data flows from EU/UK/Switzerland. Identify what personal data you collect and process. Assess current privacy practices.

3. Select Independent Recourse Mechanism

Choose a dispute resolution provider like BBB National Programs or JAMS. Register with your chosen provider.

4. Draft DPF-Compliant Privacy Policy

Create or update privacy policy to include all required elements: DPF participation, data types, individual rights, complaint mechanisms, IRM details.

5. Choose Frameworks & Data Types

Decide: EU-U.S., UK Extension, and/or Swiss-U.S. DPF. Specify: HR Data, Non-HR Data, or both.

6. Register on DPF Website

Create account at dataprivacyframework.gov. Complete self-certification application. Upload privacy policy draft.

7. Pay Certification Fee

Submit payment based on annual revenue tier ($250-$4,500). Fee covers initial certification and first year.

8. Department of Commerce Review

Wait for DoC to review your submission (1-3 weeks). Respond to any requests for revisions. Update privacy policy as needed.

9. Publish Approved Privacy Policy

Post approved privacy policy on your public website. Notify DoC of publication and URL.

10. Certification Active

Company appears on public DPF List. Certification is valid for one year. Mark calendar for annual re-certification.

Real-World Example: RudderStack's DPF Journey

RudderStack, a warehouse-native customer data platform serving companies like Crate&Barrel and Allbirds, achieved DPF certification in November 2024. The certification process—which typically takes 4-6 weeks—enabled them to streamline data transfers for their rapidly growing European customer base.

Why it mattered: As RudderStack's CEO Soumyadeb Mitra explained, the certification "reduces compliance burden for customers and strengthens trust with organizations that rely on us to safeguard their information." Rather than negotiating Standard Contractual Clauses for each EU partner, they can now onboard European customers faster while maintaining the same high privacy standards.

Coverage: All three frameworks (EU-U.S., UK Extension, Swiss-U.S.) • Timeline: Completed in standard 4-6 week window • Result: Simplified procurement for enterprise customers across Europe
